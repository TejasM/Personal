Notes on Jobs to Be Done Framework

At the beginning of 2016, my startup, SharpScholar, was going through tumultuous times. We were attempting to pivot around in the classroom teaching and enrichment industry. From an engineering perspective I was having trouble coping with constant pivoting with strategy and sought out alternate approaches to business development.

This is when I came across the Jobs To Be Done Framework. In particular, I came across the version by Tony Ulwick the founder of StrategyN. Below are my notes on JTBD and one approach about how to apply it:

  • Failing fast and pivoting are ineffective as they are essentially trying find unmet needs through solutions - what about problem interview approach through lean canvas?

  • Know the 15 unmet needs of the market upfront

  • Research so deep that the solution is pretty apparent

  • Segment the market around unmet needs:

    • Don't segment around behaviour, age, etc.

    • What is a need? How do you define a need?

    • Desire to outcome needs

    • Need statements shouldn't have the solution or process ideas

  • Job Map

  • Break down jobs into steps

  • Each step will have a desire outcome to be measured

  • Define jobs in a functional way (not emotional that is the job of sales and marketing)

  • Identifying Unmet Needs Metrics

    • Functional metrics

    • Financial metrics

    • Consumption change metrics

      • Maintaining, Upgrade, etc.

    • Who has those has those metrics?

      • Functional:

        • Teacher

      • Functional:

        • Students? Teacher?

        • Administrators?

      • Consumption Metrics:

        • IT Administrators

  • Opportunity = Importance - 2*Satisfaction or Opportunity = Importance + max(Importance – Satisfaction, 0)

  • Purchase and Buying purposes are different

  • Purchase process is emotional, don't let that affect the product

    • Except in terms of perception as functional value

  • Personas:

    • Different process, market segment != Different needs

    • Focus on people who struggle more getting the same job done

    • Use as a use case

  • Apple: 20% market size - 90% profit size

  • Find underserved large needs for people who will pay

    • Eg. Specific type of specialty jobs such as a specific type of surgery for surgeons

  • The job not the product is the unit of analysis:

    • The outcome of the individual job are the units of metric

    • The algorithm of evaluation is: Opportunity = Importance + max(Importance – Satisfaction, 0)

  • After identifying the opportunity innovation happens through one of the following six:

    • Add features to an existing platform to help customers get the core job done better.

    • Add features to an existing platform to help customers get one or more related jobs done.

    • Create a new platform to help customers get the core job done better and/or cheaper.

    • Create a new platform to help customers get the core job better and also get one or more related jobs done.

    • Create a new platform that enables a new job executor to execute the core job.

    • Create a new platform that enables a new job executor to execute the core and related jobs
